We evaluate FedSat under realistic federated heterogeneity and provide quantitative evidence that the two proposed components—confusion-calibrated cross-entropy (CACS/C3E) on clients and class-specialized top-$p$ aggregation on the server—improve global accuracy and per-class balance. Reported values are taken from the last available row of each run's test statistics.

\subsection{Experimental setup}
- Datasets and non-IID splits. We use CIFAR-100, EMNIST, and FMNIST with Dirichlet label-skew partitions. Unless noted, runs use 100 clients, batch size 64, and $E{=}5$ local epochs.
- Metric. Final global test accuracy (\texttt{global\_accuracy}) at the last round. Per-class diagnostics are provided in the supplementary material.
- Methods. CACS maintains an EMA-based soft-confusion estimator to shape the local objective; FedSat selects the top-$p$ globally struggling classes using a compact per-class signal during aggregation.

\section{Experiments}
We evaluate FedSat under realistic federated heterogeneity and provide quantitative evidence that the two proposed components—confusion-calibrated cross-entropy (CACS/C3E) on clients and class-specialized top-$p$ aggregation on the server—improve global accuracy and per-class balance. Reported values are taken from the last available row of each run's test statistics.

\subsection{Experimental setup}
- Datasets and non-IID splits. We use CIFAR-100, EMNIST, and FMNIST with Dirichlet label-skew partitions. Unless noted, runs use 100 clients, batch size 64, and $E{=}5$ local epochs.

\subsection{Per-class balance and top-$p$ sensitivity}
Per-class recall diagnostics indicate that CACS reduces persistent asymmetric confusions at the client level; FedSat then focuses aggregation on globally under-performing classes, improving tail-class recall. A sweep over top-$p$ shows stable gains for moderate $p$, while overly small $p$ can over-focus and degrade global accuracy.

\subsection{Overheads and reproducibility}
FedSat transmits only a compact per-class vector in addition to model parameters; the client-side cost is a light EMA update. We observed negligible wall-clock overhead per local epoch. Detailed logs and plots are provided as supplementary artifacts.

\subsection{Summary}
The evidence supports our claims: (1) confusion-calibrated local learning (CACS/C3E) yields consistent accuracy gains under label skew and missing-class regimes, and (2) struggler-aware, top-$p$ aggregation further prioritizes the hardest classes, improving global accuracy and per-class balance without additional communication.
